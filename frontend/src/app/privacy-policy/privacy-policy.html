2 What Information We Collect

2.1   Account & Authentication Information

When you register or sign in we collect and store:

  • Email address — used as your account identifier and to deliver price-drop notifications.
  • Display name / username — shown in the interface.
  • User ID — an internal identifier used to link your alerts to your account. Never shared externally.
  • Authentication tokens — stored locally in your browser and used solely to keep you signed in. Never transmitted to third parties.
Matches Chrome Web Store declaration: Authentication information

2.2   Price Alert Data

When you create a price alert we store:

  • Product identifier — to know which product to monitor.
  • Target price or drop percentage — the condition you set for notification.
  • Alert status — whether the alert is active or paused.
  • Your account ID — to link the alert to your account.

This data is stored on our private server.

2.3   Email Notification Content

When a price alert is triggered, we send an email to the address registered on your account. The email contains the product name, old price, new price, and a link to the product. We do not store the content of outgoing emails beyond the delivery logs kept by our transactional email provider (Resend) for up to 30 days.

Matches Chrome Web Store declaration: Personal communications (emails)

2.4   Product Price Data (Scraped, Not Personal)

We collect publicly available product and price data from the Tesco Hungary website. This data includes product names, prices, and identifiers. It is not personal data and is not linked to any individual user.

2.5   Browser Extension Local Storage

The browser extension stores the following data exclusively on your device:

  • Language and theme preference
  • Product name cache — a small local cache used to display product names without extra network requests
  • Authentication tokens — used to keep you signed in, as described in §2.1 above

None of this data is transmitted to us or any third party, except that your login token is sent to our own API to authenticate your requests.
